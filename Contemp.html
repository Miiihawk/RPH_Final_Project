This era is about the Philippines continuing to grow and face modern challenges. Governments have focused on improving the economy, building infrastructure, and addressing social issues. 1998 to 2001 - Joseph Estrada's Presidency and EDSA II

Joseph Estrada, a former actor turned populist politician, became president in 1998, winning with a wide margin. His administration initially focused on poverty alleviation and rural development but was soon engulfed in scandals, including allegations of plunder. Estrada faced impeachment in 2000 after a whistleblower accused him of receiving millions of pesos in illegal gambling payoffs.

The trial was halted when senators allied with Estrada voted against opening incriminating evidence, triggering mass protests. This culminated in EDSA II, a peaceful revolution from January 17–20, 2001, led by civil society groups, students, and military factions. Estrada was forced to resign, and Vice President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo assumed the presidency.

2001 - EDSA II

EDSA II, from January 17–20, 2001, toppled Joseph Estrada's presidency following allegations of corruption and plunder. Unlike EDSA I, which united a broad coalition, EDSA II was driven primarily by middle-class protests and civil society, with significant military involvement. Estrada was forced to resign, paving the way for Gloria Macapagal Arroyo's presidency.

2001 - EDSA III

EDSA III occurred in May 2001, shortly after Estrada's arrest. Estrada's supporters, largely from marginalized sectors, rallied for his release and demanded Arroyo's resignation. Violent clashes marked this uprising, which was less peaceful and less supported by elites compared to the previous revolutions.

2001 to 2010 - Gloria Macapagal Arroyo's Administration

Gloria Macapagal Arroyo's presidency was marked by economic growth, focusing on policies that promoted exports, infrastructure, and the IT industry. However, her administration faced allegations of election fraud in the "Hello Garci" scandal during the 2004 presidential elections.

Arroyo also survived EDSA III in May 2001, a protest largely composed of Estrada supporters demanding her resignation. Although less peaceful than EDSA I and II, it showcased the enduring political divide among Filipinos. Despite controversies, Arroyo launched the Strong Republic Nautical Highway to improve trade connectivity and tourism.

2010 to 2016 - Benigno "Noynoy" Aquino III Administration

Benigno Aquino III ran on an anti-corruption platform, with his campaign slogan "Daang Matuwid" (Straight Path) His administration oversaw economic growth, with the Philippines achieving investment-grade credit ratings. Aquino championed the Bangsamoro Basic Law as part of peace efforts with Muslim separatists but faced setbacks, including the deadly Mamasapano clash in 2015, where 44 Special Action Force (SAF) officers were killed.

Aquino also initiated the case against China in the Permanent Court of Arbitration regarding the West Philippine Sea, resulting in a favorable ruling for the Philippines in 2016.

2016 to 2022 - Rodrigo Duterte's Presidency

Rodrigo Duterte's administration was defined by his controversial war on drugs, which resulted in thousands of extrajudicial killings and drew international condemnation. Duterte adopted a pivot to China, fostering closer ties despite territorial disputes in the South China Sea.

The Build, Build, Build program, his flagship infrastructure initiative, included highways, airports, and railways to boost economic growth. Duterte also faced criticism for his handling of the COVID-19 pandemic, with allegations of mismanagement and inadequate healthcare response.

2022 to Present - Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr.'s Presidency

Ferdinand Marcos Jr., son of former dictator Ferdinand Marcos Sr., won the 2022 presidential election, marking a significant political comeback for the Marcos family. His administration focuses on economic recovery post-COVID-19, with emphasis on digital transformation and infrastructure. However, his presidency is shadowed by his family's controversial legacy, particularly issues surrounding wealth amassed during his father's regime.
