1972 - Declaration of Martial Law

On September 21, 1972, President Ferdinand Marcos declared Martial Law under Proclamation No. 1081, citing threats from communist insurgents and Muslim separatists. This move dissolved Congress, suspended civil liberties, and allowed Marcos to rule by decree. The media were censored, and opposition figures, including Senator Benigno Aquino Jr., were arrested. This period saw increased militarization and the emergence of "crony capitalism," benefiting Marcos' allies.

1973 - Ratification of the Constitution

A new Constitution was ratified through a highly controversial referendum. This document replaced the presidential system with a parliamentary one, allowing Marcos to extend his rule indefinitely. Critics argued that the plebiscite was conducted under duress and widespread manipulation.

Mid-1970s - Crony Capitalism and Economic Decline

Marcos' government favored close allies and family members in economic ventures, leading to monopolies and widespread corruption. Infrastructure projects were heavily funded but often came with allegations of overpricing and kickbacks. Economic mismanagement, compounded by the global oil crisis, led to rising poverty.

1981 - Formal Lifting of Martial Law

Martial Law was officially lifted in 1981, but Marcos retained authoritarian powers through decrees. The period was marked by allegations of electoral fraud, particularly during the presidential election that year, which Marcos won amid accusations of manipulation.

Close

1983 - Assassination of Benigno Aquino Jr.

On August 21, 1983, opposition leader Benigno Aquino Jr. was assassinated upon his return from exile. His death galvanized the opposition and sparked nationwide protests, laying the groundwork for the People Power Revolution.

Close

1986 - People Power Revolution

A snap presidential election in February 1986, marred by allegations of fraud, led to widespread protests. The People Power Revolution at EDSA forced Marcos to flee to Hawaii. Corazon Aquino, widow of Benigno Aquino Jr., assumed the presidency, symbolizing the restoration of democracy.
